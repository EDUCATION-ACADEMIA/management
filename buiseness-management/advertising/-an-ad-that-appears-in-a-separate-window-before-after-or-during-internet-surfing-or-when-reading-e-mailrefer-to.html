-An ad that appears in a separate window before, after, or during Internet surfing or when reading e-mail.refer to?

(A) ** Pop-up Ad

(B) Pop-under Ad

(C) URL Listing

(D) Interstitials

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -While pop-ups are displayed on top of the websites you’re visiting, the difference for pop-under ads is the windows are opened in the background. You can see them when you close your current browser window or site.

Concept note-2: -Banners are portable backdrop displays. Pop up stands are seamless backdrop displays. Banners are ideal for filling smaller stand spaces and make great accompanying displays to smaller table top set ups. They’re also becoming increasingly popular for displaying promotional messages and offers in retail environments.

Concept note-3: -Pop-up ads or pop-ups are forms of online advertising on the World Wide Web. A pop-up is a graphical user interface (GUI) display area, usually a small window, that suddenly appears ("pops up") in the foreground of the visual interface.

Concept note-4: -The window that “pops up” when you select a function from a menu is officially known as a “dialog box.” The first popups were DOS terminate and stay resident programs in the 1980s (see TSR). See popup menu.
