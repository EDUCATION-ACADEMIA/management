ADVERTISING

[SOURCES]
Organization negotiates with a commercial concern-such as a sports governing body or film distributor-in order to show that company’s products on television or radio.

(A) Advertising

(B) Personal Selling

(C) merchandising

(D) ** broadcasting rights

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Broadcasting rights (often also called media rights) are rights which a broadcasting organization negotiates with a commercial concern-such as a sports governing body or film distributor-in order to show that company’s products on television or radio, either live, delayed or highlights.

Concept note-2: –(1) Every broadcasting organisation shall have a special right to be known as ‘’broadcast reproduction right” in respect of its broadcasts.” (2) The broadcast reproduction right shall subsist until twenty-five years from the beginning of the calendar year next following the year in which the broadcast is made.

Concept note-3: -broadcasting, electronic transmission of radio and television signals that are intended for general public reception, as distinguished from private signals that are directed to specific receivers.

Concept note-4: -The field of broadcasting includes both government-managed services such as public radio, community radio and public television, and private commercial radio and commercial television.

Concept note-5: -Broadcasting is part of right to freedom of speech and expression provided by Article 19(1) of the Indian Constitution and various Supreme Court decision like Secretary, Ministry of Information and Broadcasting v.
