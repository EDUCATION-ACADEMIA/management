Ideas/thoughts about a group or category of people that includes the belief that all members of that group share certain characteristics.

(A) endorsement

(B) ** stereotype

(C) sponsorship

(D) target audience

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If someone is stereotyped as something, people form a fixed general idea or image of them, so that it is assumed that they will behave in a particular way. He was stereotyped by some as a rebel. [

Concept note-2: -A stereotype is a widely held, simplified, and essentialist belief about a specific group. Groups are often stereotyped on the basis of sex, gender identity, race and ethnicity, nationality, age, socioeconomic status, language, and so forth. Stereotypes are deeply embedded within social institutions and wider culture.

Concept note-3: -Etymology. The term stereotype comes from the French adjective stéréotype and derives from the Greek words (stereos), “firm, solid” and (typos), impression, hence “solid impression on one or more ideas/theories."

Concept note-4: -A stereotype is a mistaken idea or belief many people have about a thing or group that is based upon how they look on the outside, which may be untrue or only partly true. Stereotyping people is a type of prejudice because what is on the outside is a small part of who a person is.
