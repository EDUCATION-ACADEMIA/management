The type of appeal which is related to a person’s psychological and social needs for purchasing products and services?

(A) Rational appeal

(B) ** Emotional appeal

(C) Moral appeal

(D) Humour appeal

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The goal is to stimulate emotions in order to appeal to the psychological, emotional, or social needs of the consumer.

Concept note-2: -Consumer psychology is a specialty area that studies how our thoughts, beliefs, feelings, and perceptions influence how we buy and relate to goods and services. Consumer psychologists investigate how the decision-making process, social persuasion, and motivation influence why shoppers buy some things but not others.

Concept note-3: -Psychological appeals are triggers that highlight specific aspects of an object that a person finds interesting or attractive. In “The Experience Effect, ” Jim Joseph lists a great overview of how both rational and emotional benefits are attached to everyday products.

Concept note-4: -Rational Appeal This type of appeal persuades consumers by stimulating their emotional, social, or psychological needs. Rational appeals work by convincing the target audience about the benefits of purchasing the product or service.

Concept note-5: -The rational approach uses marketing to try to prove the product’s quality and usefulness by listing the product’s benefits, or quoting facts or statistics. By contrast, emotive marketing instead appeals directly to a consumer’s emotional state, needs and aspirations.
