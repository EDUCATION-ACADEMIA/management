When developing an advertisement, what element of design focuses on the degree of lightness and darkness?

(A) Shape

(B) Line

(C) Form

(D) ** Value

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The degree of difference between any two elements of ad design is called contrast. Think thick and thin lines, small and large fonts, and dark and light colors. It’s a powerful tool to make the ad pop, bring focus to a particular object, and create new meaning. But it has to be used sparingly.

Concept note-2: -Value: Refers to the degree of lightness or darkness in a color.

Concept note-3: -Value is one of the seven elements of art. Value deals with the lightness or darkness of a color. Since we see objects and understand objects because of how dark or light they are, value is incredible important to art.

Concept note-4: -A three-pronged element of art: hue, value, and intensity. Hue is the color itself. Value is the hue’s lightness or darkness and changes when white or black is added to it. Intensity is the aspect of brightness and purity of a color.

Concept note-5: -Value: In design, value refers to the lightness or darkness of a color. The values of a color are often visualized in a gradient, which displays a series of variations on one hue, arranged from the lightest to the darkest.
