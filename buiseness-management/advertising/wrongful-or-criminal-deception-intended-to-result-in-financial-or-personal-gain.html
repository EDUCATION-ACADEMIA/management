Concept note-1: -He contends that the standard definition of fraud is: wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain; or a person or thing intended to deceive others, typically by unjustifiably claiming or being credited with accomplishments or qualities.

Concept note-2: -Fraud is an intentionally deceptive action designed to provide the perpetrator with an unlawful gain or to deny a right to a victim. Types of fraud include tax fraud, credit card fraud, wire fraud, securities fraud, and bankruptcy fraud.

Concept note-3: -Fraud is defined as the wrongful or criminal deception intended to result in financial or personal gain. Fraud includes false representation of fact, making false statements, or by concealment of information.

Concept note-4: -Fraud is deception deliberately committed for an unlawful gain. This type of scam is a criminal offense that can lead to significant financial losses for an organization.

Concept note-5: -A fraud is an intentional deception made for personal gain or to damage another individual. The specific legal definition varies by legal jurisdiction. Fraud is a crime, and is also a civil law violation.
