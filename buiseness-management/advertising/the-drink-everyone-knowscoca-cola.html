The drink everyone knows:Coca-Cola.

(A) ** Bandwagon

(B) Avant-garde

(C) Facts and Figures

(D) Testimonial

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: The phrase ‘the drink everybody knows’ convinces buyers to purchase the product since others are also buying them which is an example of bandwagon propaganda. The bandwagon appeal emphasizes that “everyone else is doing it, and so should you."

Concept note-2: -Bandwagon is when people try to attract more people into doing/buying something simply because everyone else is doing it too. The picture says, "The drink everybody loves". This is bandwagon because it says that everyone loves the coke product so more people should buy it.

Concept note-3: -Expert-Verified Answer 2] Name calling or stereotyping propoganda technique is being used.

Concept note-4: -BAND WAGON: This common propaganda method is when the speaker tries to convince us to accept their point of view or else we will miss out on something really good. The Band-Wagon technique is often used in advertising. Examples: “This is the wave of the future", “Be the first on your block", “Act Now!".

Concept note-5: -Using them has been described as “name-calling in reverse.” Examples of words commonly employed as glittering generalities in political discourse include freedom, security, tradition, change, and prosperity.
