The author tries to appeal to readers’ feelings of sympathy with the example of the

(A) trip to Rome

(B) ** rescued dog

(C) sugary cereal

(D) professional hypnotist

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ethos is about establishing your authority to speak on the subject, logos is your logical argument for your point and pathos is your attempt to sway an audience emotionally. Leith has a great example for summarizing what the three look like. Ethos: ‘Buy my old car because I’m Tom Magliozzi.

Concept note-2: -Rhetorical appeals are the qualities of an argument that make it truly persuasive. To make a convincing argument, a writer appeals to a reader in several ways. The four different types of persuasive appeals are logos, ethos, pathos, and kairos.

Concept note-3: -Logos appeals to the audience’s reason, building up logical arguments. Ethos appeals to the speaker’s status or authority, making the audience more likely to trust them. Pathos appeals to the emotions, trying to make the audience feel angry or sympathetic, for example.
