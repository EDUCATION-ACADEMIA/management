BUISENESS MANAGEMENT

ADVERTISING

[SOURCES]
A person is made to have strong feelings about a situation orproduct

(A) testimonial

(B) ** appeals to emotion

(C) expert opinion

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Pathos, or the appeal to emotion, means to persuade an audience by purposely evoking certain emotions to make them feel the way the author wants them to feel. Authors make deliberate word choices, use meaningful language, and use examples and stories that evoke emotion.

Concept note-2: -An emotional appeal uses emotions as the basis of an argument’s position without factual evidence that logically supports the major ideas endorsed by the presenter. In an emotional appeal, persuasive language is used to develop the foundation of an appeal to emotion-based arguments instead of facts.

Concept note-3: -Examples of appeals to emotion. An example of the appeal to emotion is the following argument: “They’re saying that what I did was a crime, but I’m not guilty, because if I am then I’ll have to spend many horrible years in jail”.

Concept note-4: -And right there, you have the four Emotional Magnets: Safety, Value, Experience, and Achievement. I find it helpful to remember the acronym, S.A.V.E. for the four emotional needs (probably because they literally saved my life).
