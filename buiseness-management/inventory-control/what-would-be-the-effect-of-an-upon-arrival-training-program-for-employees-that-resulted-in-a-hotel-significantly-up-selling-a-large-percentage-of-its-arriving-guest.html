What would be the effect of an upon-arrival training program for employees that resulted in a hotel significantly up selling a large percentage of its arriving guest?

(A) ADR and Occupancy would increase, and RevPAR would be unchanged

(B) RevPAR and Occupancy would increase, and ADR would be unchanged

(C) ** ADR and RevPAR would increase and occupancy % would be unchanged

(D) ADR and RevPAR would decrease and occupancy % would increase

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It acts as an indicator of the hotel’s overall performance and profits. ADR helps hotel owners determine the average rate of the rooms sold over a specific period of time. This duration can be variable – it may be 30-days, a quarter, or even a year.

Concept note-2: -The ADR formula is: Room revenue / Number of rooms sold. Just remember to exclude any complimentary rooms or rooms occupied by staff members. ADR is important because it’s one of the primary metrics used to help you gauge the success of your hotel and how you measure against your competition.

Concept note-3: -Many other factors can affect your occupancy rate, including room rates, the guest experience, your online reputation, room cleanliness and the quality and availability of your facilities.

Concept note-4: -Revenue per available room (RevPAR) – A metric used to assess how well a hotel has managed their inventory and rates to optimize revenue. Calculated by multiplying occupancy by ADR.
