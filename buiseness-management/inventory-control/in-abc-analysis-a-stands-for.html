In ABC analysis A stands for

(A) Components having high cost and high usage

(B) Components having low cost and low usage

(C) ** Components having high cost and low usage

(D) Components having low cost and high usage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -ABC analysis is a method in which inventory is divided into three categories, i.e. A, B, and C in descending value. The items in the A category have the highest value, B category items are of lower value than A, and C category items have the lowest value. Inventory control and management are critical for a business.

Concept note-2: -ABC (Always Better Control) analysis is one of the most commonly used inventory management methods. ABC analysis groups items into three categories (A, B, and C) based on their level of value within a business.

Concept note-3: -Activity-based costing (ABC) is a method of assigning overhead and indirect costs-such as salaries and utilities-to products and services. The ABC system of cost accounting is based on activities, which are considered any event, unit of work, or task with a specific goal.

Concept note-4: -’B’ items – 15% of the annual inventory value of your items (likely made up of 30% of your items) ‘C’ items – 5% of the annual inventory value of your items (likely made up of 50% of your items)

Concept note-5: -ABC analysis is an approach for classifying inventory items based on the items’ consumption values. Consumption value is the total value of an item consumed over a specified time period, for example a year.
