Define what is meant by authority

(A) The number of levels of authority that are in the chain of command

(B) Number of subordinates who report directly to the line manager

(C) Workers that a line manager is responsible for

(D) ** The power that one person has to make decisions and control what other workers do

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Authority can be defined as the legal right of a person or superior to command his subordinates. On the other hand, accountability refers to the duty of an individual to carry out his performance as per the company standards. The direction of the flow of authority is from the superiors to subordinates.

Concept note-2: -Power is the capacity of a person to influence others and alter their actions, beliefs, and behaviors. However, it’s important to note that there is a difference between power vs. influence. Authority is the legitimate power that a person or group is granted to practice over others within an organization.

Concept note-3: -the power or right to control, judge, or prohibit the actions of others. (often plural) a person or group of people having this power, such as a government, police force, etc. a position that commands such a power or right (often in the phrase in authority)

Concept note-4: -Authority is a form of legitimate power that comes with a certain position. It is based on control and credibility. Being in a position of authority does not automatically inspire your team or give you devotion. On the other hand, leadership is based on the personal qualities and character of an individual.

Concept note-5: -Authority refers to the legal and formal right of a person or someone in a superior position to control the decision-making and direct the actions or tasks carried out by their subordinates.
