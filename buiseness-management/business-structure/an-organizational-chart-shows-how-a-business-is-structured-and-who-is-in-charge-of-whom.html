An organizational chart shows how a business is structured and who is in charge of whom.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An organizational chart graphically represents an organization’s structure, highlighting the different jobs, departments, and responsibilities that connect the company’s employees to each other and to the management team.

Concept note-2: -An organizational chart shows the internal structure of an organization or company. The employees and positions are represented by boxes or other shapes, sometimes including photos, contact information, email and page links, icons and illustrations. Straight or elbowed lines link the levels together.

Concept note-3: -An organizational chart, also called organigram, organogram, or organizational breakdown structure (OBS) is a diagram that shows the structure of an organization and the relationships and relative ranks of its parts and positions/jobs.

Concept note-4: -Each role carries a certain level of responsibility, and managers can establish workflows to help keep the business running smoothly. Company owners or executives usually create an organization chart once the business gets big enough to need the graphical representation that an organization chart provides.

Concept note-5: -2) Yes, the organization structure appears on the organization chart. It is exhibited on the organizational chart for the purpose of making employees aware about the authority and responsibility, reporting relationship among departments and individuals and flow of information and order.
