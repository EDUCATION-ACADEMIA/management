[SOURCES]
Shows who is allowed to give instructions and who will receive them.

(A) Span of Control

(B) ** Chain of Command

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A chain of command is an organizational structure that documents how each member of a company reports to one another. At the top of the chart would be the founder, owner or CEO, and the people who report to them would appear directly below.

Concept note-2: -A chain of command is a ranking of authority where senior people in an organization direct and control employees who are below them. it is used to direct or control reporting interconnection from the bottom to the top.

Concept note-3: -A chain of command describes who employees should report to and when they should consult their supervisor with project decisions. Not only is it part and parcel of a company’s hierarchy, but it’s essential to creating an efficient and enjoyable work environment for your employees.

Concept note-4: -Establishing a Chain of Command A company’s hierarchy starts with the CEO at the top. Following the CEO are the vice president and upper management employees who report directly to the CEO. Then, there are department managers and supervisors who report to the higher-level executives.
