What is a subordinate?

(A) ** A member of staff below a manager in the chain of command

(B) A manager in a business

(C) A member of staff at the bottom of a business

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A chain of command structure typically consists of three distinct levels: top-level management (CEO, COO), middle-level management (director-level positions) and front-line staff (those who interact with customers and carry out day-to-day operations).

Concept note-2: -In a business setting, a subordinate is simply an employee who is not of management level and responds to either a manager or a group of managers. It is also possible that there is a hierarchy within the business where a manager is the subordinate of a higher-level manager.

Concept note-3: -Establishing a Chain of Command A company’s hierarchy starts with the CEO at the top. Following the CEO are the vice president and upper management employees who report directly to the CEO. Then, there are department managers and supervisors who report to the higher-level executives.

Concept note-4: -Employees will generally report directly to those who are listed above them on the org chart, and the most popular, tried-and-tested way of building these reporting relationships is through a corporate chain of command, also known as the “hierarchical structure”.
