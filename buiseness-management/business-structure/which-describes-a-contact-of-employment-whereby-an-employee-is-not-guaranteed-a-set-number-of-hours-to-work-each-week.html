Which describes a contact of employment whereby an employee is not guaranteed a set number of hours to work each week?

(A) Temporary

(B) ** Flexible hours

(C) Part-time

(D) Full-time

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Flexible working hours refers to the schedule which allows employees to start and finish their workday when they want. This means that employees can come to work earlier or later than the set time.

Concept note-2: -A flexible working definition is simply to say it’s a working method with changeable start and finish times. It can also include remote working. Some typical types of flexible working include: Arranging some work from home days to provide a better work-life balance.

Concept note-3: -Casual Employment Contracts Contracts for casual employment are usually employed when employees’ hours are not guaranteed. In a standard arrangement, the employer can promise a specific amount of hours, but they could extend the hours should they be required.

Concept note-4: -Definition and Example of a Flexible Work Schedule For example, an employee might be allowed to arrive between 9-11 a.m. and leave between 5-7 p.m. Or they might be able to work four days a week, if they work longer hours on those days. Working remotely can also be an option when companies offer flexible schedules.
