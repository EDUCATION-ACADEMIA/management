Which describes a contract of employment whereby a person is contracted by a business to complete a single task?

(A) Full-time

(B) Temporary

(C) Part-time

(D) ** Freelance

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is a Freelance Contract? A contract for freelancers is an agreement between you and your client(s) that outlines everything you and your client need to know about the job. Specifically, it’s a legal document that, should it become necessary, can be used in court.

Concept note-2: -Freelancing is a type of self-employment. Instead of being employed by a company, freelancers tend to work as self-employed, delivering their services on a contract or project basis.

Concept note-3: -There are three basic types of freelance contracts that you can consider: the buyout, work for hire and rights managed. When you’re creating intellectual property, copyright is involved. Copyright and usage rights must be transferred in writing. So you need a contract of some sort in order to transfer any rights.

Concept note-4: -Unlike a contractor, an employer cannot dictate when, where and how a freelancer works. Freelancers can set their own timings and plan their own work days, as long as they complete the deliverables by the agreed upon deadline.
