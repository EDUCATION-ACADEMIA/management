Which of the following statements best describes the term span of control?

(A) The formal channels of communication in an organisation

(B) Removal of a level in an organisational structure

(C) The number of levels in an organisational hierarchy

(D) ** The number of people that directly report to a line manager

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Number of subordinates a manager can effectively direct. It is also called span of management. It is the number of subordinates who report directly to a manager or leader and can be effectively managed by him. The more employees assigned to a manager, the wider their span of control.

Concept note-2: -Span of control (or span of management) is the number of subordinates who report directly to a manager or leader. The more employees assigned to a manager, the wider their span of control.

Concept note-3: -Span of control refers to the number of individuals or resources that one supervisor can manage effectively during an incident. The optimal span of control is one supervisor to five subordinates (1:5). However, effective incident management may require ratios significantly different from this.

Concept note-4: -Elliott Jaques has the view-point that a manager may have up to as many immediate sub-ordinates that he can know personally in the sense that he can assess personal effectiveness. 5. Haimann and Scott defined span of control as the number of subordinates who can be effectively supervised and managed.

Concept note-5: -Span of control refers to the number of subordinates that can be managed effectively and efficiently by supervisors or managers in an organization. Typically, it is either narrow or wide resulting in a flatter or more hierarchical organizational structure. Each type has its inherent advantages and disadvantages.
