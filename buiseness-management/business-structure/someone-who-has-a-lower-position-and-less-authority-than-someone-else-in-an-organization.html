Someone who has a lower position and less authority than someone else in an organization.

(A) ** subordinate

(B) decentralized

(C) handoffs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -noun. /səˈbɔːdɪnət/ /səˈbɔːrdɪnət/ a person who has a position with less authority and power than somebody else in an organization synonym inferior.

Concept note-2: -A subordinate is someone who works for someone else. As a verb, to subordinate means to place or rank one thing below another. When you’re doing a group project, sometimes you have to subordinate your ideas to the desires of the larger group. The prefix sub-means “lower” and ordinate refers to an ordering of things.

Concept note-3: -If someone is your subordinate, they have a less important position than you in the organization that you both work for.

Concept note-4: -Who is a subordinate? A subordinate is someone who is in a secondary rank. In a business setting, a subordinate is simply an employee who is not of management level and responds to either a manager or a group of managers.

Concept note-5: -: placed in or occupying a lower rank, class, or position. : submissive to or controlled by authority. subordinate.
