Which of the following is not a long term asset?

(A) building

(B) equipment

(C) machinery

(D) ** accounts receivable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Accounts receivable are considered a current asset because they usually convert into cash within one year. When a receivable takes longer than one year to convert, it will be recorded as a long-term asset. In addition to accounts receivable, there are other current assets found on the balance sheet.

Concept note-2: -Accounting divides your company assets into two classes: current and long-term. Current assets include cash and anything you use up or convert to cash over the next 12 months. Typical examples are supplies or accounts receivable. Anything you plan to keep beyond a year is a long-term asset.

Concept note-3: -Current assets refer to those that are liquid, meaning they can be easily converted to cash in less than a year. Accounts receivable are typically collected in two months or less. For this reason, they are considered a current asset or a “short-term asset.”
