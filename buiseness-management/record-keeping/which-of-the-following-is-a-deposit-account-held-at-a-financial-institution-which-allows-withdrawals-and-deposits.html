Which of the following is a deposit account held at a financial institution which allows withdrawals and deposits?

(A) ** Checking account

(B) Savings accoung

(C) Money market account

(D) Certificate of deposit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A current account, also called a demand deposit account, is a basic checking account. Consumers deposit money and the deposited money can be withdrawn as the account holder desires on demand.

Concept note-2: -A checking account is a deposit account held at a financial institution that allows withdrawals and deposits. Also called demand accounts or transactional accounts, checking accounts are very liquid and can be accessed using checks, automated teller machines (ATMs), and electronic debits, among other methods.

Concept note-3: -A savings account in a bank allows you to deposit and withdraw money any time you want.

Concept note-4: -A non-interest bearing peso account, also known as Checking or Demand Deposit Account wherein deposits are made over-the-counter, while withdrawals are made through the issuance of a check.

Concept note-5: -These accounts also have what we call the overdraft facility. For the convenience of the accountholders banks also allow withdrawal of amounts in excess of the balance of the deposit. This facility is known as an overdraft facility.
