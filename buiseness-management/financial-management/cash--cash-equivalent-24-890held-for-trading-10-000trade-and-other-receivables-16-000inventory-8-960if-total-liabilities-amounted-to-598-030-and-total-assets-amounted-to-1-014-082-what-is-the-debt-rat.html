EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cash and Cash Equivalents are entered as current assets on a company’s balance sheet. The total value of cash and cash equivalents is calculated by adding together the total of all cash accounts and any highly liquid investments that can be easily converted into cash that qualify as a cash equivalent.

Concept note-2: -Accounts Receivable DO NOT fall under the category of Cash Equivalents.

Concept note-3: -Current assets include cash, cash equivalents, accounts receivable, stock inventory, marketable securities, pre-paid liabilities, and other liquid assets. The Current Assets account is important because it demonstrates a company’s short-term liquidity and ability to pay its short-term obligations.
