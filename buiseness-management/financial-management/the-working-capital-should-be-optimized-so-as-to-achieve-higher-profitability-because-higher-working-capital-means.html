The Working Capital should be optimized so as to achieve higher profitability because higher working capital means

(A) ** Higher interest cost

(B) Risk of disturbance of the operating cycle

(C) Lower interest cost

(D) All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Working capital here refers to the current assets less current liabilities (net working capital). It should be optimized because higher working capital means higher interest cost and lower working capital means a risk of disturbance of the operating cycle.

Concept note-2: -Reduce inventory and increase inventory turnover Well-managed inventory management may be the most powerful leverage to working capital improvements. Achieving a higher net working capital calculation can be achieved by reducing slow-moving inventory, increasing the inventory turnover cycles, and avoiding stockpiling.

Concept note-3: -What is the working capital optimization cycle? The working capital optimization cycle is a way of looking at a companyâ€™s receivables, payables and inventory and at how it handles those on a day-to-day basis. The cycle provides a look at how much working capital it takes to run your business.

Concept note-4: -While a high level of working capital may indicate improved liquidity, operational efficiency, and increased profits, it is equally important to ensure that much-needed funds required for growth are not trapped in working capital.

Concept note-5: -Working capital affects both the liquidity as well as profitability of a business. As the amount of working capital increases the liquidity of the business increases. However since current assets offer low return with the increase in working capital the profitability of the business falls.
