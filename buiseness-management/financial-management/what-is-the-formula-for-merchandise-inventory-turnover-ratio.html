What is the formula for Merchandise Inventory Turnover Ratio?

(A) Average Merchandise Inventory/Cost of Goods Sold

(B) Cost of Goods Sold/Merchandise Inventory

(C) Merchandise Inventory/Cost of Goods Sold

(D) ** Cost of Goods Sold/Average Merchandise Inventory

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The inventory turnover ratio is calculated by dividing the cost of goods by average inventory for the same period. A higher ratio tends to point to strong sales and a lower one to weak sales.

Concept note-2: -The average inventory formula is: Average inventory = (Beginning inventory + Ending inventory) / 2.

Concept note-3: -To calculate the inventory turnover ratio, divide your businessâ€™s cost of goods sold by its average inventory.

Concept note-4: -You can calculate the inventory turnover ratio by dividing the inventory days ratio by 365 and flipping the ratio. In this example, inventory turnover ratio = 1 / (73/365) = 5. This means the company can sell and replace its stock of goods five times a year.
