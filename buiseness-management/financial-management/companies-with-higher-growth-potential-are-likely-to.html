Companies with higher growth potential are likely to

(A) ** pay low dividend

(B) Pay higher dividends

(C) Dividends are not affected by growth consideration

(D) Dividends may be affected

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Companies with higher growth opportunities tend to retain more money out of their earnings so as to finance the required investments. Therefore, higher growth prospects result in lower dividend payment.

Concept note-2: -Companies with higher growth rate are likely to pay lower rate of dividend. Companies with higher growth rate would prefer to plough back its profit for the growth of the company which would result in long term benefits rather than distributing dividends which is temporary.

Concept note-3: -Companies who are having the higher growth pattern are likely to pay lower dividends.

Concept note-4: -A company having higher and stable earnings can declare higher dividends than a company with lower and unstable earnings.

Concept note-5: -If the shareholders prefer regular income, how does this affect the dividend decision: It will lead to payment of dividend. It is the indicator to retain more earnings.
