____ is the study of how money is used by individuals, societies and nations to meet people’s needs.

(A) Debt

(B) Income

(C) ** Economics

(D) Debit

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Economics is the study of how people allocate scarce resources for production, distribution, and consumption, both individually and collectively.

Concept note-2: -Economics is the study of scarcity and its implications for the use of resources, production of goods and services, growth of production and welfare over time, and a great variety of other complex issues of vital concern to society.

Concept note-3: -The Monetary Economics Program studies the conduct and effects of monetary policy, including its impact on interest rates and inflation, and the consequences of policy actions by central banks. It also considers macroeconomic forces that impinge on central bank decision-making.

Concept note-4: -Definition: Microeconomics is the study of individuals, households and firms’ behavior in decision making and allocation of resources. It generally applies to markets of goods and services and deals with individual and economic issues.

Concept note-5: -economics. the study of how individuals and societies choose to allocate scarce resources.
