Each of the four sides of a swimming pool measures 12 meters. The pool is 6 meters deep. How much water will be needed to fill it completely? Use your calculator to calculate using exponents.

(A) 144 cubic meters

(B) ** 864 cubic meters

(C) 72 cubic meters

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The pool is 6 meters deep. How much water will be needed to fill it completely? Use your calculator to calculate using exponents. A) 144 cubic meters.

Concept note-2: -Answer:-24m² is the area of swimming pool.

Concept note-3: -To get the average depth of your pool, take the depth of the shallow end of your pool and add it to the depth of the deep end, and divide by two. Here’s a quick example. Let’s say the shallow end of your pool is 3 feet deep and the deep end is 7 feet deep. Add those two together 3 feet +7 feet =10 feet.

Concept note-4: -Length x width x average depth x 7.5 = volume (in gallons) Length times width gives the surface area of the pool. Multiplying that by the average depth gives the volume in cubic meters. If you’d like to find the pool volume in gallons, multiply your results by 7.5, as there are 7.5 gallons for each cubic foot.

Concept note-5: -The average swimming pool takes 18, 000-20, 000 gallons of water to fill.
