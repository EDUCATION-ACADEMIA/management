In his traditional role, the finance manager is responsible for

(A) Proper utilisation of funds

(B) ** Arrangement of financial resources

(C) Acquiring capital assets of the organisation

(D) Efficinet management of capital

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Explanation: In his traditional role the finance manager is responsible for arrangement of financial resources. Financial managers are responsible for the financial health of an organization.

Concept note-2: -By definition, a financial manager is someone who oversees the financial health of an organisation and helps ensure financial sustainability. They supervise many important functions such as monitoring cash flow, managing expenses, producing accurate financial data, and strategising for profit.

Concept note-3: -The traditional finance manager was given a target amount of funds to be raised and was given the responsibility of procuring these funds. So, his function was for raising the funds only. Once the funds were procured his function was over.

Concept note-4: -Financial management helps organisations to plan, organise, and govern financial activities to keep business moving and maintain healthy profits. A key role of financial managers is decision-making, which takes into account the businessâ€™ short-term and long-term goals.

Concept note-5: -The roles and responsibilities for financial management and financial performance in public sector entities are clear. The Public Finance Act 1989 states that the chief executive of a government department is responsible for the financial management and financial performance of the department.
