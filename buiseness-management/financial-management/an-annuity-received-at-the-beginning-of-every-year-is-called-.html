An annuity received at the beginning of every year is called ____

(A) Annuity Factor

(B) Regular Annuity

(C) ** Annuity Due

(D) Deferred Annuity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An annuity due is an annuity in which the cash flows, or payments, occur at the beginning of the period. An annuity due is also called an annuity in arrears. The cash flows occur at the beginning of years 1 through 5.

Concept note-2: -An annuity-due is an annuity whose payments are made at the beginning of each period. Deposits in savings, rent or lease payments, and insurance premiums are examples of annuities due. Each annuity payment is allowed to compound for one extra period.

Concept note-3: -An ordinary annuity is a series of regular payments made at the end of each period, such as monthly or quarterly. In an annuity due, by contrast, payments are made at the beginning of each period. Consistent quarterly stock dividends are one example of an ordinary annuity; monthly rent is an example of an annuity due.

Concept note-4: -If the periodic payments are made at the end of each period, the annuity is called an immediate annuity or ordinary annuity.
