This term means that differences can occur from one service provider to the next.

(A) ** Heterogeneous

(B) Intangible

(C) Inseparable

(D) Perishable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Heterogeneity of Services Heterogeneity means that the service quality varies from one provider or location to another. This characteristic explains the difference in prices depending on location and quality of services.

Concept note-2: -Knowledge heterogeneity refers to differences in customers’ knowledge levels regarding how their needs can be satisfied while relationship heterogeneity reflects differences in customers’ propensities to engage in relationships with firms.

Concept note-3: -Inseparability refers to the fact that the production of a service cannot be separated from its consumption by customers. Perishability means unused service capacity of one time period cannot be stored for future use. Heterogeneity is variation in service quality.

Concept note-4: -For example, at a salon parlor, each customer coming demands a different haircut. The barber cannot cut every person’s hair in the same way. They have to listen to his customer; otherwise, they would lose a customer. This is how heterogeneity in the service market is seen.
