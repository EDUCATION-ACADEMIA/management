A product with a well-developed image uses the same brand name in different product categories. What type of strategy is this?

(A) An industrial branding strategy

(B) A mixed branding strategy

(C) A co-branding strategy

(D) ** A brand extension strategy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Brand extension or brand stretching is a marketing strategy in which a firm marketing a product with a well-developed image uses the same brand name in a different product category. The new product is called a spin-off.

Concept note-2: -The strategy behind a brand extension is to use the companyâ€™s already established brand equity to help it launch its newest product. The company relies on the brand loyalty of its current customers, which it hopes will make them more receptive to new offerings from the same brand.

Concept note-3: -Co-branding is a marketing strategy that utilizes multiple brand names on a good or service as part of a strategic alliance. Co-branding can boost the reputation of two or more brands, depending on the strategy employed.

Concept note-4: -Umbrella branding (also known as family branding) is a marketing practice involving the use of a single brand name for the sale of two or more related products. Umbrella branding is mainly used by companies with a positive brand equity (value of a brand in a certain marketplace).

Concept note-5: -A company has a multi-brand strategy when its portfolio of products has distinct brands or names. For example, Nestle has a multi-brand strategy with over 2000 brands including KitKat and Nespresso.
