Capital mentioned in the Balance Sheet of a company can be verified from ____

(A) Chartered Accountant

(B) Company

(C) ** Balance Sheet available with Registrar of companies

(D) None of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Share capital is shown on Equity & Liabilities side of balance sheet in company‘s accounts.

Concept note-2: -The balance sheet provides information on a company’s resources (assets) and its sources of capital (equity and liabilities/debt). This information helps an analyst assess a company’s ability to pay for its near-term operating needs, meet future debt obligations, and make distributions to owners.

Concept note-3: -The balance sheet is broken into two main areas. Assets are on the top or left, and below them or to the right are the company’s liabilities and shareholders’ equity. A balance sheet is also always in balance, where the value of the assets equals the combined value of the liabilities and shareholders’ equity.

Concept note-4: -Reserve Capital is not shown under the heading “Share Capital” in a balance sheet.
