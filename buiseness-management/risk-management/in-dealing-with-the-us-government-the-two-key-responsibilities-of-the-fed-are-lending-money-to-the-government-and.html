In dealing with the U.S. government, the two key responsibilities of the Fed are lending money to the government and

(A) ** Controlling the nation’s paper currency

(B) Reviewing congresstional spending.

(C) Developing the federal budget

(D) Producing goverment payroll checks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How the Fed Helps the Economy. The Federal Reserve acts as the U.S. central bank, and in that role performs three primary functions: maintaining an effective, reliable payment system; supervising and regulating bank operations; and establishing monetary policies.

Concept note-2: -As the Federal Reserve conducts monetary policy, it influences employment and inflation primarily through using its policy tools to influence the availability and cost of credit in the economy.

Concept note-3: -The Fed controls the supply of money by increasing or decreasing the monetary base. The monetary base is related to the size of the Fed’s balance sheet; specifically, it is currency in circulation plus the deposit balances that depository institutions hold with the Federal Reserve.
