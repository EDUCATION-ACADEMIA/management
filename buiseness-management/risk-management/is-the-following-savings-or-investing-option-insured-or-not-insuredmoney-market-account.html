Is the following savings or investing option Insured or Not Insured?Money Market Account

(A) ** Insured

(B) Not Insured

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yes. Like other deposit accounts, money market accounts are insured by the FDIC and NCUA up to $250, 000 for each account holder.

Concept note-2: -Many people use investment products to help buy a home, send children to college, or build a retirement nest egg. But unlike traditional checking or savings accounts, non-deposit investment products are not insured by the FDIC, even if they were purchased from an FDIC-insured bank.

Concept note-3: -Basically, all demand-deposit accounts that become general obligations of the bank are covered by the FDIC. The type of accounts that can be FDIC-insured include negotiable orders of withdrawal (NOW), checking, savings, and money market deposit accounts, as well as certificates of deposit (CDs).
