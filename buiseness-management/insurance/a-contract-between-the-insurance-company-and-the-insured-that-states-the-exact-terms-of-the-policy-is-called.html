A contract between the insurance company and the insured that states the exact terms of the policy is called:

(A) ** policy

(B) premium

(C) insurance

(D) risk

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An insurance policy is a legal contract between the insurance company (the insurer) and the person(s), business, or entity being insured (the insured). Reading your policy helps you verify that the policy meets your needs and that you understand your and the insurance companyâ€™s responsibilities if a loss occurs.

Concept note-2: -The Principle of Indemnity The insurer (provider) compensates the insured (policyholder). The insurance company promises to compensate the policyholder for the amount of the loss up to the amount agreed upon in the contract.

Concept note-3: -An insurance contract issued by one insurer (the reinsurer) to compensate another insurer (the cedant) for losses on one or more contracts issued by the cedant. The party that has an obligation under a reinsurance contract to compensate a cedant if an insured event occurs.

Concept note-4: -A document containing terms and conditions of insurance contract is called policy.
