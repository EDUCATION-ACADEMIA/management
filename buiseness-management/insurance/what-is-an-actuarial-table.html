What is an actuarial table?

(A) ** A table of statistical data, derived using calculus and the laws of probability, that shows the likelihood of a risk, such as death, occurring to an individual in a certain profile

(B) an insurance policy that protects a business if employees. products, or services cause property damage or bodily injury to a third party

(C) insurance that protects businesses if their property is, for example, stolen or damaged by fire

(D) A mathematical principal underlying the reasoning that the greater the number of companies insured, the more likely that the insurer can predict the percentage and amount of losses over a given period of time

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Actuarial tables (also called life expectancy tables, mortality tables, and life tables) are statistical tools used by companies, scientists, courts, and government agencies to predict the life expectancy of a person by their age, gender, and other factors.

Concept note-2: -An actuarial life table is a table that displays the probability that a person of specific age will die before their next birthday. The tables are in spreadsheet form and essentially demonstrate the probability of death for each age group within a population.

Concept note-3: -Actuarial science applies probability analysis and statistics to define, analyze, and solve the financial impact of uncertain future events. Actuarial science helps insurance companies forecast the probability of an event occurring to determine the funds needed to pay claims.

Concept note-4: -An actuarial life table is a table or spreadsheet that shows the probability of a person at a certain age dying before their next birthday.
