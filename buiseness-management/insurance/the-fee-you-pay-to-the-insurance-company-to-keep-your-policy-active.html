The fee you pay to the insurance company to keep your policy active:

(A) deductible

(B) claim

(C) liability

(D) ** premium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The insurance company stipulates that an individual or business periodically pay them a specific amount of money as premium for the availing and maintenance of their insurance policy and coverage. Insurance companies consider many factors while determining the premiums, particularly in case of life insurance.

Concept note-2: -Premium-The amount paid by an insured to an insurance company to obtain or maintain an insurance policy.

Concept note-3: -A premium is the amount you pay an insurer for insurance cover. It reflects what the insurer believes is the likelihood you will make a claim. It also includes an insurerâ€™s business costs, and may also reflect the benefits of any discounts or bonuses the insurer may offer to you.

Concept note-4: -The amount you pay for your health insurance every month. In addition to your premium, you usually have to pay other costs for your health care, including a deductible, copayments, and coinsurance. If you have a Marketplace health plan, you may be able to lower your costs with a premium tax credit.

Concept note-5: -An insurance premium is an amount that every insurance policyholder has to pay; in return for which, the insurers provide coverage against the losses as per the policy.
