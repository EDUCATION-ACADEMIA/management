Protects drivers from people who cannot pay insurance claims

(A) Miscellaneous coverage

(B) medical payments coverage

(C) property damage liability coverage

(D) ** uninsured/ underinsured motorists protection

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Uninsured motorist coverage protects you if you’re hit by a driver who has no auto insurance. Underinsured motorist coverage, which is usually offered alongside uninsured motorist coverage, protects you if you’re hit by a driver who doesn’t have enough coverage to pay for the damages or injuries they caused.

Concept note-2: -Uninsured Motorist Protection: This type of insurance protects you if you are involved in a road accident and the at-fault driver that has caused you and your vehicle damages does not carry the third-party liability insurance policy.

Concept note-3: -Uninsured Motorist (UM) insurance – Also known as Uninsured Motorist Bodily Injury (UMBI) insurance, this coverage will pay your and your passengers’ medical bills if you’re involved in an accident with an uninsured motorist who is at fault.

Concept note-4: -You never know when the person who hits you will have insurance or enough insurance to pay for your injuries and damage to your vehicle. Uninsured motorist coverage also pays if you’re in a hit-and-run accident and the other driver can’t be found to pay for damages.

Concept note-5: -Uninsured motorist coverage, referred to as UM or UMBI, is a type of car insurance that can pay for medical expenses if you or your passengers are injured in an auto accident caused by: A driver who doesn’t have liability car insurance.
