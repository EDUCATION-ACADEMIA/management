The transfer of an insurance policy by an insured to another person. Any claims arising from the transferred policy passes to the new policyholder.

(A) ** Assignment

(B) Beneficiary

(C) Insured

(D) Actuary

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Definition: An absolute assignment is the act of complete transfer of the ownership (all rights, benefits and liabilities) of the policy completely to other party without any terms and condition. Description: Absolute assignment shifts the ownership of the insurance policy.

Concept note-2: -Assignment of the policy refers to the transfer of rights, title, and policy ownership from the policyholder to another person or entity. The person involved in assigning/transferring the policy is called assignor, and the person/institution to which it is assigned is called the assignee.

Concept note-3: -Absolute Assignment: Complete rights and ownership of the policy transferred to the Assignee. Conditional Assignment: Rights and ownership of the policy under certain conditions to the Assignee.
