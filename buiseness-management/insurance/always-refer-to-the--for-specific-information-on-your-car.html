(C) garage

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It explains issues, such as the check engine light, wiper usage, turning signals, cruise control, blind-spot information systems, airbags, safety belts, and hazard lights. Therefore, before you drive your car, read the manual.

Concept note-2: -Owner’s manuals usually cover three main areas: a description of the location and operation of all controls; a schedule and descriptions of maintenance required, both by the owner and by a mechanic; and specifications such as oil and fuel capacity and part numbers of light bulbs used.

Concept note-3: -The vehicle owner’s manual is an instructional booklet that is specific to your vehicle. It includes important information to make you a better driver and car owner, including maintenance and troubleshooting advice.

Concept note-4: -Details of any hazardous materials used. Health and safety information relating to operation and maintenance. Health and safety information relating to removal or dismantling. Location of services e.g. hidden services.
