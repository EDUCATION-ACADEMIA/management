Under the principle ____, the takaful operator are only liable to the loss caused by and insured perils and not loss caused by uninsured or excepted perils.

(A) Insurable Interest

(B) Indemnity

(C) Contribution

(D) ** Proximate Cause

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The principle of proximate cause is the cause that is primary to the occurred event. It could also be the most significant incident which cascades into the loss event. The insurer will entertain the claim only if this significant cause is close enough to the loss.

Concept note-2: -The Principle of Proximate Cause If the proximate cause is one in which the property is insured against, then the insurer must pay compensation. If it is not a cause the property is insured against, then the insurer doesnâ€™t have to pay.

Concept note-3: -It means that if the proximate cause of the loss is insured then the insurer is liable to pay the compensation to the insured. This article was all about the topic of the Principle of Causa Proxima, which is an important topic in Business Studies for Commerce students.

Concept note-4: -Proximate cause is the cause having the most significant impact in bringing about the loss under a first-party property insurance policy, when two or more independent perils operate at the same time (i.e., concurrently) to produce a loss.
