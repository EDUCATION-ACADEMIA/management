INSURANCE

[SOURCES]
The amount the insured pays on a claim before the insurer pays the remainder

(A) Premium

(B) Peril

(C) Replacement Cost

(D) ** Deductible

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The word ‘Deductible’ is closely associated with insurance and it is the amount of money that you must pay before the insurer begins to cover the rest of the claim amount. How it works: If your insurance plan’s deductible is Rs. 50, 000, you will pay 100% of the eligible expenses until the bills total Rs. 50, 000.

Concept note-2: -You’re responsible for your policy’s stated deductible every time you file a claim. After you pay the car deductible amount, your insurer will cover the remaining cost to repair or replace your vehicle. Example: You have a $500 deductible and $3, 000 in damage from a covered accident.

Concept note-3: -Deductible is the amount that a policy holder has to pay before the insurance company starts paying up. In other words, the insurance company is liable to pay the claim amount only when it exceeds the deductible.

Concept note-4: -Definition: Premium is an amount paid periodically to the insurer by the insured for covering his risk. Description: In an insurance contract, the risk is transferred from the insured to the insurer. For taking this risk, the insurer charges an amount called the premium.

Concept note-5: -A deductible is the fixed amount that policyholders have to bear before their insurance policies start contributing and cover the larger portion of your medical bill. With larger copay amount, policyholders are liable to pay lesser premiums. Deductibles also allow policyholders to pay smaller premium amounts.
