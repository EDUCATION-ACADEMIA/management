Contract between an individual and an insurer specifying the terms.

(A) Risk

(B) Insurance

(C) ** Policy

(D) Premium

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Life insurance is a contract between an insurer and a policy owner. A life insurance policy guarantees that the insurer pays a sum of money to named beneficiaries when the insured dies in exchange for the premiums paid by the policyholder during their lifetime.

Concept note-2: -An insurance policy is a legal contract between the insurance company (the insurer) and the person(s), business, or entity being insured (the insured). Reading your policy helps you verify that the policy meets your needs and that you understand your and the insurance companyâ€™s responsibilities if a loss occurs.

Concept note-3: -1) An insurance policy is a contract between the insurer and the insured. 2) The insured is the person whose life is being covered against the risk under the policy. 3) The insurer is the insurance company that provides the insurance cover.

Concept note-4: -The insurer (provider) compensates the insured (policyholder). The insurance company promises to compensate the policyholder for the amount of the loss up to the amount agreed upon in the contract.

Concept note-5: -Insurance is a legal agreement between two parties i.e. the insurance company (insurer) and the individual (insured). In this, the insurance company promises to make good the losses of the insured on happening of the insured contingency. The contingency is the event which causes a loss.
