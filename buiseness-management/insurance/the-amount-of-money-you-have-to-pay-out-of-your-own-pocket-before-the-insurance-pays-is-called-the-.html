The amount of money you have to pay out of your own pocket before the insurance pays is called the ____

(A) Premium

(B) Estimate

(C) ** Deductible

(D) Co-Pay

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Deductible. Meaning. Copayment is the amount of your health insurance claim that you have to bear from your own pocket. A deductible in health insurance is the fixed amount of money that your medical expenses must exceed in order for your health insurance plan to be usable. Form.

Concept note-2: -Deductible is the amount that a policy holder has to pay before the insurance company starts paying up. In other words, the insurance company is liable to pay the claim amount only when it exceeds the deductible.

Concept note-3: -A deductible is the amount of money you need to pay before your insurance begins to pay according to the terms of your policy. An out-of-pocket maximum refers to the cap, or limit, on the amount of money you have to pay for covered services per plan year before your insurance covers 100% of the cost of services.

Concept note-4: -Your expenses for medical care that aren’t reimbursed by insurance. Out-of-pocket costs include deductibles, coinsurance, and copayments for covered services plus all costs for services that aren’t covered.

Concept note-5: -Deductibles is a fixed sum of money that policyholders are required to pay before their insurance policy starts contributing to their medical treatment.
