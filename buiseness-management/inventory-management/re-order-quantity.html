Re-order quantity

(A) ** The number of unit order each time

(B) The number of unit order in maximum quantity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reorder Quantity = ADU x ALT. Once you multiply ADU and ALT, you’ll know the amount of inventory you need to reorder. Let’s look at an example of how to calculate reorder quantity for an individual product: ADU = 10 units.

Concept note-2: -It is also known as reorder point. The reorder quantity is the quantity of the order that is to be placed on a new purchase order for the particular item.

Concept note-3: -Reorder point = total lead time demand + safety stock You’ll need to understand how to calculate your lead time demand and safety stock before using ROP, which, thankfully, is also fairly simple.

Concept note-4: -Re-Order Level implies the level at which new order should be placed for the purpose of restoration of stock. Re-Order Quantity implies the size of the lot for which the order is placed when the stock of material falls to reorder level. When to buy or place an order for materials.
