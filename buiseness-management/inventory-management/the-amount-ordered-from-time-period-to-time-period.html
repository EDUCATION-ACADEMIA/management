[SOURCES]
The amount ordered from time period to time period.

(A) Inventory Order System

(B) ** Reorder Quantity

(C) Safety Stock

(D) Inventory Management

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reorder Quantity = ADU x ALT. Once you multiply ADU and ALT, you’ll know the amount of inventory you need to reorder. Let’s look at an example of how to calculate reorder quantity for an individual product: ADU = 10 units.

Concept note-2: -Reorder quantity, also known as the reorder point, is the quantity of an order to be placed for purchasing a specific item that needs to be restocked. It is based on the supplier’s average replenishment period, the item’s maximum usage during the replenishment interval and the need for safety stock.

Concept note-3: -The average time taken by the supplier to deliver the order to the factory’s stores is the average reorder period.

Concept note-4: -How do you calculate reorder point with example? A simple reorder point calculation is daily unit sales multiplied by lead time between inventory order and its arrival in days, plus safety stock.

Concept note-5: -To calculate the reorder level, multiply the average daily usage rate by the lead time in days for an inventory item.
