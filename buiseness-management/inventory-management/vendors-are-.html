Vendors are ____

(A) the branch of government that gives financial help.

(B) the people that buy stocks into your company.

(C) ** the businesses that sell you inventory.

(D) The employees that get taxes taken out.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A vendor is a general term for anyone who buys and sells goods or services. A vendor purchases products and services and then sells them to another company or individual. Large retailers, like Target, rely on many different vendors to supply products, which it buys at wholesale prices and sells at higher retail prices.

Concept note-2: -A vendor is a person or company that sells goods or services for a profit. They can operate in a business-to-consumer (B2C) or business-to-business (B2B) environment. In B2B, vendors are often known as suppliers.

Concept note-3: -A vendor definition refers to an individual or business selling or supplying commodities and services to end-users. They deliver raw materials to manufacturers and finished goods to wholesalers, retailers, and customers as part of the supply chain management process.

Concept note-4: -Definition and Example of a Vendor A company or an individual who sells a good or a service is a vendor. Vendors may sell to other businesses, or they may be retailers who sell straight to consumers. An example of a vendor is a company that provides inventory for boutique clothing stores.

Concept note-5: -Vendor managed inventory (VMI) is an arrangement where suppliers manage inventory levels that have been pre-determined. In short, the supplier takes decisions on behalf of the retailer wherein the supplier replenishes the inventory continuously.
