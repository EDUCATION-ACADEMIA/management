The main purpose of establishing a reorder point is to:

(A) avoid purchasing too much inventory if projections are incorrect

(B) ** avoid a stockout situation

(C) maintain adequate anticipation stock

(D) reduce the company’s carrying costs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Setting a reorder point helps you optimize your inventory, replenish your stock of individual items at the right time, and meet your market demand without going out of stock.

Concept note-2: -Reorder levels are critical for efficient inventory management. Not only does it help you reduce carrying costs, but it also helps prevent both overstocking and understocking, and ensures you have adequate stock to meet customer demand so that you don’t lose sales.

Concept note-3: -The reorder point (ROP) is the level of inventory which triggers an action to replenish that particular inventory stock. It is a minimum amount of an item which a firm holds in stock, such that, when stock falls to this amount, the item must be reordered.
