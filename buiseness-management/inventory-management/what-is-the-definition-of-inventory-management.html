EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is Inventory Management? Inventory management refers to the process of ordering, storing, using, and selling a company’s inventory. This includes the management of raw materials, components, and finished products, as well as warehousing and processing of such items.

Concept note-2: -Inventory management, a critical element of the supply chain, is the tracking of inventory from manufacturers to warehouses and from these facilities to a point of sale. The goal of inventory management is to have the right products in the right place at the right time.

Concept note-3: -Just-in-time, or JIT, is an inventory management method in which goods are received from suppliers only as they are needed. The main objective of this method is to reduce inventory holding costs and increase inventory turnover.

Concept note-4: -Warehouse inventory management is a process that involves receiving, storing, and tracking inventory in a warehouse; managing warehouse staff; and optimizing storage space and costs; all of which directly impacts fulfillment, shipping, and the customer experience.

Concept note-5: -The 4 Types of Inventory Management The types of inventory management are Raw Materials, Works-In-Process, Maintenance, Repair and Operations or MRO and Finished Goods.
