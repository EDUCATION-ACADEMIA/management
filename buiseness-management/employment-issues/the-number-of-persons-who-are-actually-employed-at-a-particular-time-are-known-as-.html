The number of persons who are actually employed at a particular time are known as-

(A) Labour Force

(B) ** Work Force

(C) Child Labour

(D) Agriculture force

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Description: The labour force participation rate is the measure to evaluate working-age population in an economy. The participation rate refers to the total number of people or individuals who are currently employed or in search of a job.

Concept note-2: -The number of persons, who are actually employed at a particular time are known as work force. Workforce means the number of persons, who are actually employed at a particular time. Labour Force is the total of employed and unemployed persons.

Concept note-3: -Persons “in the labor force” are those in the civilian noninstitutional population, age sixteen years or older, who are employed or who are unemployed and seeking employment.

Concept note-4: -The Worker-Population ratio is defined as the number of employed people per thousand people.

Concept note-5: -Yes, it is true as the labour force is the number of people who are employed plus the unemployed who are looking for work. The workforce is the total number of people actually working.
