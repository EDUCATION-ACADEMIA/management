What is the definition of business analysis?

(A) A list of tasks a Business Analysis must do.

(B) ** Practice of enabling change in an enterprise by defing needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders.

(C) The set of techniques used to work as a liaison among stakeholders in order to understand the structure, policies and operations of an organization.

(D) The entire task a Project Manager carries out.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The International Institute of Business Analysis defines Business Analysis as “the practice of enabling change in an organizational context, by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders."

Concept note-2: -Business analysis is the practice of enabling change in an enterprise by defining needs and recommending solutions that deliver value to stakeholders.

Concept note-3: -Business analysis aimed at identifying business needs, opportunities and problems, and then find the right solutions. It could be about improving information systems, improving business processes and / or organizational improvements.

Concept note-4: -Requirements Management Plan: Defines the process to be followed in managing the solution scope and requirements.

Concept note-5: -The business analyst is inherently a stakeholder in all business analysis activities. The BABOK® Guide presumes that the business analyst is responsible and accountable for the execution of these activities.
