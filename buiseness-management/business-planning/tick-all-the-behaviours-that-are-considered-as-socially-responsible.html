(A) ** Reduce the emission of CO2 in production

(B) Use child labours

(C) Require employees to work overtime without extra pay

(D) ** Check whether the suppliers of material meet sustainability standards

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Social responsibility is an ethical theory in which individuals are accountable for fulfilling their civic duty, and the actions of an individual must benefit the whole of society. In this way, there must be a balance between economic growth and the welfare of society and the environment.

Concept note-2: -CSR is generally categorized in four ways: environmental responsibility, ethical/human rights responsibility, philanthropic responsibility and economic responsibility.

Concept note-3: -Working for the community, such as volunteering, giving blood donations, and working at a food bank or animal shelter. Supporting issues that affect society, such as advocating political or social issues that can help others-for example, advocating for child labor laws, purchasing fair trade products, recycling.

Concept note-4: -It is about working together – across government, civil society, and communities – to improve the lives of millions of people by making growth more inclusive.
