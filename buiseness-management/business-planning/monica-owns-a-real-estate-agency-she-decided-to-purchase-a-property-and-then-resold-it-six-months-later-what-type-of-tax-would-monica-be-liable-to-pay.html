Monica owns a real estate agency. She decided to purchase a property and then resold it six months later. What type of tax would Monica be liable to pay?

(A) Group tax

(B) ** Capital gains tax

(C) Fringe benefits tax

(D) Goods and services tax

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The LTCG Tax is applicable when a particular property is sold after 24 months of buying it. The time period was reduced from 3 years to 2 years in Budget 2017. The rate of LTCG Tax is 20%. This is over and above the regular income tax payable by the seller, on the income earned through salary or business profit.

Concept note-2: -Currently, the long term capital gain tax rate on property is set at 20% with the addition of cess and surcharge.

Concept note-4: -The short-term capital gains would attract a tax at the rate of 15% of the investor decides to sell it within a year. A long-term Mutual Funds capital gains tax would be charged at a rate of 10% on profits exceeding Rs. 1 Lakh generated through equity-oriented funds and shares.
