The total amount of money earned before deductions or taxes are taken.

(A) ** Gross Pay

(B) Allowance

(C) Wages

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What is Gross Income? Gross income refers to the total income earned by an individual on a paycheck before taxes and other deductions. It comprises all incomes received by an individual from all sources – including wages, rental income, interest income, and dividends.

Concept note-2: -Gross Salary is employee provident fund (EPF) and gratuity subtracted from the Cost to Company (CTC). To put it in simpler terms, Gross Salary is the amount paid before deduction of taxes or other deductions and is inclusive of bonuses, over-time pay, holiday pay, and other differentials.

Concept note-3: -By subtracting all the eligible deductions from the gross taxable income, you will arrive at your total income on which you need to pay tax basis your tax slab. This slab rate is different for senior citizens. For those who are over 60 years old with up to Rs 3 lakh net income, the tax rate is nil.

Concept note-4: -Aggregate income before deductions under chapter VIA is known as Gross Total Income.
