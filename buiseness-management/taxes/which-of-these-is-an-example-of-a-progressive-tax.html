Which of these is an example of a progressive tax?

(A) ** One where higher income earners have a higher percentage of their income taxed than lower income earners

(B) One where higher and lower income earners lose the same percentage of their income to taxes

(C) One where lower income earners have a higher percentage of their income taxed than higher income earners

(D) A tax proposed by the Democratic Party, as opposed to the Republican Party

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Under a progressive tax system, the tax rate people pay increases as their income increases. This means that people with higher incomes get taxed at a higher rate than those with lower incomes do. The U.S. federal income tax is an example of a progressive tax system.

Concept note-2: -If one tax code has a low rate of 10% and a high rate of 30%, and another tax code has tax rates ranging from 10% to 80%, the latter is more progressive. Income Tax, Luxury Sales Tax, Estate tax and surcharge on net income beyond Rs 50 Lakhs are a few examples of Progressive tax.

Concept note-3: -What is a Progressive Tax. A Progressive tax is where taxes increase in line with incomes. In other words, the higher the income, the higher the rate of taxation. For example, someone earning $20, 000 a year may pay 10 percent in taxes, whilst someone else earning $80, 000 will pay 30 percent.

Concept note-4: -regressive tax-A tax that takes a larger percentage of income from low-income groups than from high-income groups.

Concept note-5: -Definition: Progressive tax is the taxing mechanism in which the taxing authority charges more taxes as the income of the taxpayer increases. A higher tax is collected from the taxpayers who earn more and lower taxes from taxpayers earning less.
