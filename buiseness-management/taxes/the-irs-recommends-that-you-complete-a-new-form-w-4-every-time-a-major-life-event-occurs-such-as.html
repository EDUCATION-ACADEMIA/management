BUISENESS MANAGEMENT

TAXES

[SOURCES]
The IRS recommends that you complete a new Form W-4 every time a major life event occurs, such as

(A) birth of a child

(B) a spouse gaining or losing a job

(C) ** all above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Form W-4 claiming exemption from withholding is valid for only the calendar year in which it’s furnished to the employer. To continue to be exempt from withholding in the next year, an employee must give you a new Form W-4 claiming exempt status by February 15 of that year.

Concept note-2: -There is no limit on how often you can update your W-4 during the year to keep up with changes in your situation.

Concept note-3: -You Don’t Need to Submit a W-4 Form Every Year You’re not required to periodically submit a new W-4 form. However, if you start a new job, you’re required to complete a W-4 form at that time. That’s the only way your new employer will know how much federal income tax to withhold from your wages.

Concept note-4: -No. Employees who have furnished Form W-4 in any year before 2020 are not required to furnish a new form merely because of the redesign. Employers will continue to compute withholding based on the information from the employee’s most recently furnished Form W-4.
