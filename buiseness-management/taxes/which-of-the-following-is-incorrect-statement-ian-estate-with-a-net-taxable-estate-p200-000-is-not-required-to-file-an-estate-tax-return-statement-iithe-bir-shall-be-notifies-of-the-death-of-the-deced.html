Which of the following is INCORRECT? Statement I:An estate with a net taxable estate P200, 000 is not required to file an estate tax return. Statement II:The BIR shall be notifies of the death of the decedent if he has a net estate of P20, 000.

(A) Statement I only

(B) Statement II only

(C) ** Statements I and II

(D) Neither statement I nor II

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The net assets subject to taxation equal the person’s total assets minus liabilities and minus the prescribed tax-deductible portion of assets left behind by the deceased that cross some minimum threshold, below which no estate tax is levied.

Concept note-2: -"Gross estate” is a term used to describe the total dollar value of an individual’s assets at the time of their death. A gross estate value does not consider his figure debts owed and tax liabilities.

Concept note-3: -Generally, the Gross Estate does not include property owned solely by the decedent’s spouse or other individuals. Lifetime gifts that are complete (no powers or other control over the gifts are retained) are not included in the Gross Estate (but taxable gifts are used in the computation of the estate tax).

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is Sales Tax. Sales tax is an indirect tax.
