TAXES

[SOURCES]
Tim and Eileen are married and filing their taxes jointly in Nebraska. They have individual Adjusted Gross incomes of $43, 172 and $48, 912 respectively. How much do they owe in state taxes?

(A) ** $3518.76

(B) $3357.14

(C) $2521.21

(D) $3518.74

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The AGI calculation is relatively straightforward. It is equal to the total income you report that’s subject to income tax-such as earnings from your job, self-employment, dividends and interest from a bank account-minus specific deductions, or “adjustments” that you’re eligible to take.

Concept note-2: -You can find your AGI on the form you used to file your last year’s return. Various versions of Form 1040 reflect the AGI amount on different lines: Line 11 on Form 1040 and 1040-SR (2020 tax year) Line 11 on Form 1040-NR (tax year 2020 form)

Concept note-3: -The standard deduction amounts for 2022 are: Married Filing Jointly or Qualifying Widow(er) – $25, 900 (increase of $800)
