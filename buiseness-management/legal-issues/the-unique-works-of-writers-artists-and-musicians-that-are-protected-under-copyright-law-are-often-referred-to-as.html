The unique works of writers, artists, and musicians that are protected under copyright law are often referred to as

(A) patents

(B) ** intellectual property

(C) trademarks

(D) tangible property

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Copyright (or authorâ€™s right) is a legal term used to describe the rights that creators have over their literary and artistic works. Works covered by copyright range from books, music, paintings, sculpture, and films, to computer programs, databases, advertisements, maps, and technical drawings.

Concept note-2: -Copyright is an intellectual property right that law gives to a creator of literary, dramatic, musical, and artistic work and a producer of cinematograph films and sound recordings. It also applies to architectural works and computer program/software.

Concept note-3: -Patents, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets are valuable assets of the company and understanding how they work and how they are created is critical to knowing how to protect them.

Concept note-4: -Intellectual property (IP) refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions; literary and artistic works; designs; and symbols, names and images used in commerce.
