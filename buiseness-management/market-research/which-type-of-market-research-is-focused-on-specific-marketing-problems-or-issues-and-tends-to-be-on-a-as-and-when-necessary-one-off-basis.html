Which type of market research is focused on specific marketing problems or issues and tends to be on a ‘as and when necessary’, ‘one-off’ basis?

(A) Primary market research

(B) Continuous market research

(C) Secondary market research

(D) ** Ad hoc market research

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ad-hoc research is carried out for a specific purpose on behalf of market research clients. This type of research offers high-quality data solutions to any problem that your company may face.

Concept note-2: -Applied marketing research is conducted to address a specific marketing decision for a specific firm or organization.

Concept note-3: -Four common types of market research techniques include surveys, interviews, focus groups, and customer observation.

Concept note-4: -Ad Hoc Research is specifically designed to address a particular problem or issue. Ad hoc research is usually conducted when there is insufficient existing information. It is usually specifically conducted for and funded by a single client company and the results are proprietary to the client.

Concept note-5: -There are three types of market research-exploratory, descriptive, and causal.
