In this type of research, “Why” questions are often used, the researcher is part of the process, and information is open to a high degree of interpretation.

(A) ** Qualitative

(B) Quantitative

(C) Stratified

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -This is because these generate qualitative data through the use of open questions allowing a respondent to talk at length, choosing their own words. This helps the researcher develop a real sense of a person’s understanding of a situation.

Concept note-2: -Qualitative questions concern broad areas or more specific areas of research. However, unlike quantitative questions, qualitative research questions are adaptable, non-directional and more flexible. Qualitative research question focus on discovering, explaining, elucidating, and exploring.

Concept note-3: -Action research is conducted in order to solve a particular issue immediately, while case studies are often conducted over a longer period of time and focus more on observing and analyzing a particular ongoing phenomenon.

Concept note-4: -Descriptive research questions Descriptive survey questions are designed to uncover a respondent’s response towards a particular question or variable. Put simply, it’s the easiest way to quantify the particular variable(s) you’re interested in on a large scale.
