What type of question is most often used as a filter question? For example, if you answer “yes” on a certain question it may lead to additional questions that you would not answer if you say “no” to the first question.

(A) a rating scale

(B) a level of agreement scale

(C) a multiple choice question

(D) ** a two-choice question

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Dichotomous is generally a “Yes/No” question. It’s often a screening question to filter those who don’t fit the needs of the research.

Concept note-2: -For example, researchers may ask participants if they smoke (Filter Question 1), and if yes, if they smoke cigarettes (Filter Question 2a), or if they smoke marijuana (Filter Question 2b). Participants would then be asked follow-up questions about cigarette smoking and marijuana smoking, as appropriate.

Concept note-3: -Filter questions, otherwise known as contingency questions, are used in studies and surveys to target respondents and route them to questions that apply to them.

Concept note-4: -Filter questions have closed-ended response options that divide the sample into subgroups. They are also called screening questions. For example, “Do you own or rent your home?” Contingency questions are questions that are only to be answered by some subgroup(s) of respondents.

Concept note-5: -Multiple choice questions are the most popular survey question type. They allow your respondents to select one or more options from a list of answers that you define.
