[SOURCES]
The marketing planning process includes a situation analysis, which is useful for:

(A) Defining the business scope and served market segments.

(B) ** Understanding the environment and the market.

(C) Refining strategies and program.

(D) Pricing.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -a systematic approach to the achievement of marketing goals. Steps in the process include situation analysis; setting of objectives; strategy formulation; development of action programs; implementation; and control, review and evaluation.

Concept note-2: -What is Situational Analysis? Situational analysis is a process of examining a company and its competitors on the basis of size and share of the market, sales history including costs and profits, the use of advertising, the nature and types of customers and more.

Concept note-3: -Monitoring of the marketing plan results: The marketing plan should include the process of analyzing the current position of the organization. The organization needs to identify the strategies that are working and those that are not working.
