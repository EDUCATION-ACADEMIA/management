An account used to record the purchase of merchandise or goods intended for resale

(A) ** Purchases

(B) Sales

(C) Inventory

(D) Shipping Charges

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If merchandise are purchased on account, the accounts involved in the transaction are the purchases account and accounts payable account. The purchases account is debited and the accounts payable account is credited.

Concept note-2: -Purchases-Used to record all purchases of merchandise for resale, this is one of the largest expense accounts of a merchandiser. It is classified as a cost of goods sold account.

Concept note-3: -Under the periodic system, a temporary expense account named merchandise purchases, or simply purchases, is used to record the purchase of goods intended for resale.

Concept note-4: -Within accounting, merchandise is considered a current asset because itâ€™s usually expected to be liquidated (sold, turned into cash) within a year. When purchased, merchandise should be debited to the inventory account and credited to cash or accounts payable, depending on how the merchandise was paid for.

Concept note-5: -The purchases account is a general ledger account in which is recorded the inventory purchases of a business. This account is used to calculate the amount of inventory available for sale in a periodic inventory system.
