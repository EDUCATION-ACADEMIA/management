The owner of one or more shares of stock.

(A) subsidiary ledger

(B) share of stock

(C) ** stockholder

(D) charter

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As noted above, a shareholder is an entity that owns one or more shares in a company’s stock or mutual fund.

Concept note-2: -A shareholder can be a person, company, or organization that holds stock(s) in a given company. A shareholder must own a minimum of one share in a company’s stock or mutual fund to make them a partial owner. Shareholders typically receive declared dividends if the company does well and succeeds.

Concept note-3: -A stockholder is also known as a shareholder of a company or an individual that owns at least one share of an organisation’s capital stock. Stockholders are mostly the owner of the company and generally acquire the company’s accomplishment in the form of increased stock valuation.

Concept note-4: -Shareholders of a company are of two types – common and preferred shareholder. As their name suggests, they are the owners of a company’s common stocks. These individuals enjoy voting rights over matters concerning the company.
