Who developed the Four P’s concept?

(A) Ivan Pavlov

(B) B.F. Skinner

(C) ** E.J. McCarthy

(D) Sigmund Freud

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Edmund Jerome McCarthy (February 20, 1928 – December 3, 2015) was an American marketing professor and author. He proposed the concept of the 4 Ps marketing mix in his 1960 book Basic Marketing: A Managerial Approach, which has been one of the top textbooks in university marketing courses since its publication.

Concept note-2: -The 4P’s of marketing, also known as the producer-oriented model, have been used by marketers around the world for decades. Created by Jerome McCarthy in 1960, the 4Ps encourages a focus on Product, Price, Promotion and Place.

Concept note-3: -The marketing mix of Product, Price, Promotion, and Place was introduced to marketing education by E. Jerome McCarthy in 1960. These mnemonically easy-to-remember labels rapidly became the organizing structure for virtually all introductory marketing textbooks.

Concept note-4: -The 4 P’s marketing mix concept (later known as the 7 P’s of marketing) was introduced by Jerome McCarthy in his book: “Basic Marketing: A Managerial Approach". It refers to the thoughtfully designed blend of strategies and practices a company uses to drive business and successful product promotion.

Concept note-5: -In the 90s, Bob Lauterborn published an article in Advertising Age magazine that proposed a new marketing formula – the four C’s. The concepts focus on the wants and needs of the consumer, cost of satisfying those wants, and the convenience of buying the product. The four C’s are often referred to as the marketing mix.
