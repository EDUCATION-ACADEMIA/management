What are the accounts involved in this transaction?BZ Water Refilling Station received payment for the account of Mr. Ef Ven. BZ WRSgranted Mr. Ven a discount of PHP. 12, 350.1. Cash2. Purchase Discounts3. Sales Discounts4. Accounts Receivable5. Accounts Payable

(A) 1, 3 and 5

(B) 1 and 3

(C) ** 3 and 4

(D) 3 and 5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -How would you journalize the transaction “paid cash on account” in an expanded journal? Debit Cash and credit Accounts Payable.

Concept note-2: -The journal columns that are used when receiving cash on account are general which is credited and cash which is debited.

Concept note-3: -A journal shows in one place all the changes in a single account (T/F). A chart of accounts is a list of account titles and numbers showing the location of each account in a ledger (T/F). On a chart of accounts, all asset and expense accounts are in the same division (T/F).

Concept note-4: -Double lines are ruled across a journal’s amount columns to indicate that the totals have been verified as correct.
