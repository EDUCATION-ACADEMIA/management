When you markup a price you are trying to make sure you cover only the cost of the product.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Markup shows how much more a company’s selling price is than the amount the item costs the company. In general, the higher the markup, the more revenue a company makes. Markup is the retail price for a product minus its cost, but the margin percentage is calculated differently.

Concept note-2: -Cost-plus pricing is also known as markup pricing. It’s a pricing method where a fixed percentage is added on top of the cost it takes to produce one unit of a product (unit cost). The resulting number is the selling price of the product.

Concept note-3: -Markup (or price spread) is the difference between the selling price of a good or service and cost. It is often expressed as a percentage over the cost. A markup is added into the total cost incurred by the producer of a good or service in order to cover the costs of doing business and create a profit.
