A blend of features that satisfies a chosen market, including product, price, place, and promotion

(A) fashion market

(B) commissionaire

(C) ** marketing mix

(D) advertising

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The marketing mix, also known as the four P’s of marketing, refers to the four key elements of a marketing strategy: product, price, place and promotion.

Concept note-2: -The marketing mix in marketing strategy: Product, price, place and promotion. The marketing mix is the set of controllable, tactical marketing tools that a company uses to produce a desired response from its target market. It consists of everything that a company can do to influence demand for its product.

Concept note-3: -(Marketing mix explained) The four Ps are product, price, place, and promotion. They are an example of a “marketing mix, ” or the combined tools and methodologies used by marketers to achieve their marketing objectives.

Concept note-4: -Key Takeaways. A marketing mix often refers to E. Jerome McCarthy’s four Ps: product, price, placement, and promotion. The different elements of a marketing mix work in conjunction with one another. Consumer-centric marketing mixes incorporate a focus on customers into their approaches.

Concept note-5: -A company’s marketing mix is the combination of products, pricing, places and promotions it uses to differentiate itself from the competition. These four elements are commonly referred to as the “four Ps.” There is strong dependency between each of the Ps.
