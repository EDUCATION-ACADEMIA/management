Motion in a display is the use of motorized fixtures, mannequins and props

(A) ** TRUE

(B) FALSE

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Props are very important in the making of a display, and also in the process of creating imagery. They tell a story not only about the product, but also about the brand’s concept or the store’s, the merchandise’s visibility on the market and so on.

Concept note-2: -A prop is an item or group of items you use to tell your product’s message. It is usually placed in a window display, floor display or stand alone by an end cap. It can include a PoP display shelf or product display in your retail store. The items are solely for sales support and are not for sale.

Concept note-3: -A mannequin (also called a dummy, lay figure, or dress form) is a doll, often articulated, used by artists, tailors, dressmakers, window dressers and others, especially to display or fit clothing and show off different fabrics and textiles.

Concept note-4: -Retailing And Merchandising In Textiles And Appare Functional props: include anything that holds the merchandise such as mannequins, body forms or fixtures. Decorative props: create a mood provide a setting for the merchandise, and adds to the attractiveness of the display.
