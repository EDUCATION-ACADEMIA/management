When an employee steals money out of the cash register and continues to do so for a long period of time. This is what type of risk?

(A) Economic Risk

(B) Risk Management

(C) Natural Risk

(D) ** Human risk

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Embezzlement refers to a form of white-collar crime in which a person or entity intentionally misappropriates the assets entrusted to them.

Concept note-2: -Embezzlement occurs when someone steals or misappropriates money or property from an employer, business partner, or another person who trusted the embezzler with the asset.

Concept note-3: -Under these two main categories, there are many different types of theft, including embezzlement, shoplifting, fraud, and robbery. While all of these crimes have the same basic elements, they also have slight variations and different possible punishments if you are found guilty.

Concept note-4: -Monetary theft In businesses that deal with a lot of cash, like those within the retail industry, monetary theft is the most common type of employee theft. Examples of how this can occur include: Overcharging and pocketing the difference.
