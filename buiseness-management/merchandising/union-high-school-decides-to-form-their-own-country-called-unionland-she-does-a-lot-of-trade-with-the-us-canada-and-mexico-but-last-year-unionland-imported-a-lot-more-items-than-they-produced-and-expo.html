Union High School decides to form their own country called Unionland. She does a lot of trade with the U.S., Canada, and Mexico but last year Unionland imported a lot more items than they produced and exported to other countries. This is known as:

(A) Even Trade

(B) ** Trade Deficit

(C) Trade Surplus

(D) Bankruptcy

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In 2021, of the $276.5 billion in U.S. exports to Mexico, the top commodity sectors were Machinery and Mechanical Appliances (29.9%), Oils, Minerals, Lime, and Cement (16.0%), and Chemicals, Plastics, Rubber, and Leather goods (15.9%).

Concept note-2: -Mexico, Imports of goods and services as percentage of GDP is 37.89 %. Mexico, Exports of goods and services as percentage of GDP is 40.09 %.

Concept note-3: -Agricultural Products Avocados, tomatoes, onions and bell peppers are popular vegetable imports from Mexico.

Concept note-4: -Trade between Mexico and the United States developed during the rule of President Porfirio Diaz. He had had a long rule as the Mexican President (1876-1911). Diaz opened his country to foreign investment and this led to many American entrepreneurs investing in Mexican agriculture, mines, and ranches.
