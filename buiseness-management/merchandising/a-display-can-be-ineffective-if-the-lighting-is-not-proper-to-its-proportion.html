A display can be ineffective if the lighting is not proper to its proportion.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Appropriate lighting, without glare or shadows, can reduce eye fatigue and headaches; it can prevent workplace incidents by increasing the visibility of moving machinery and other safety hazards.

Concept note-2: -From the workers’ perspective, poor lighting at work can lead to eye-strain, fatigue, headaches, stress and accidents. On the other hand, too much light can also cause safety and health problems such as “glare” headaches and stress. Both can lead to mistakes at work, poor quality and low productivity.

Concept note-3: -Adequate lighting means a light intensity of 20 foot-candles (approximately equivalent to a 60 watt bulb at a clear distance of 5 feet).

Concept note-4: -Suggestions for the percent of light reflected off surfaces in a typical office include: Window blinds (40-50%). Walls (50% maximum). Business machines (50% maximum).
