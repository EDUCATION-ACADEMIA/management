What is a salesperson’s questions to a customer to get an indication of what needs to be done to close the deal?

(A) Open-Ended Questions

(B) Presentation

(C) Selling

(D) ** Trial Confirmation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Solution-and Feeling-Based Questions. Needs-Based Questions. Feature-Benefit Questions. Objection-Testing Questions.

Concept note-2: -Sales closing questions are phrases asked near the end of a sales negotiation. They’re used to inform the customer and help you get closer to your deal. They help give a lasting impression of the company and the product it’s offering to drive the decision-making process.

Concept note-3: -Trial closing questions are open-ended, opinion-asking questions. They enable you as the salesperson to assess where you are in the sales process and evaluate the readiness of your prospect to ask for the sale. The response you get from your trial closing question will tell you what to do next.
