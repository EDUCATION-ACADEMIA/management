(A) Fashion director

(B) Merchandise manager

(C) Retail buyer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which action is usually demonstrated during the rise stage of the fashion cycle? Retail fashion buyers order the items in quantity.

Concept note-2: -Trickle-up theory (Upward flow theory): The assumption that fashion trends start among the young or lower income groups and move upward to older or higher income groups. Style originates with the lower class and gains approval by upper class or the fashion elite.

Concept note-3: -Decline: The fourth stage of the fashion cycle when the market is saturated and popularity decreases. The fashion is overused and becomes dull and boring. As the fashion decreases in popularity, retailers mark down their prices. Promotions center around major clearance or closeout sales of the fashion.

Concept note-4: -Which type of retail format typically offered the greatest depth of merchandise selection? Discount stores: Centralized check-out counters. High volume of sales.
