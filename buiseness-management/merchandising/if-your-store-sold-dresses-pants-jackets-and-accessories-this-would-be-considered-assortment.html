If your store sold dresses, pants, jackets, and accessories, this would be considered assortment

(A) ** Breadth

(B) Depth

(C) Variety

(D) Selection

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Product breadth is all about how wide and diverse an assortment is. A retailer that sells many different types of products is considered to have a large product breadth. One example is Walmart, which sells everything from toilet paper and diapers to PJs and jackets.

Concept note-2: -The assortment width describes the range of different merchandise, the assortment depth the variety of individual product groups. If a warehouse/distribution center or retailer manages clothing, food, electrical goods and cosmetics, for example, the assortment dimension is broad.

Concept note-3: -What is Product Assortment? Product assortment refers to a range of products that a retailer or brand presents to its consumers.
