What is cost-plus pricing?

(A) This is the production cost involve in the tertiary sector

(B) This is the cost of supplies minus manufacturing processes

(C) This is the cost, that decreases as time passes

(D) ** This is the cost of manufacturing product plus a profit mark-up

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cost-plus pricing is a pricing strategy by which the selling price of a product is determined by adding a specific fixed percentage (a “markup") to the product’s unit cost. Essentially, the markup percentage is a method of generating a particular desired rate of return.

Concept note-2: -Cost-plus pricing is also known as markup pricing. It’s a pricing method where a fixed percentage is added on top of the cost it takes to produce one unit of a product (unit cost). The resulting number is the selling price of the product.

Concept note-3: -A cost-plus contract is an agreement to reimburse a company for expenses incurred plus a specific amount of profit, usually stated as a percentage of the contract’s full price.

Concept note-4: -Cost-plus pricing, also known as markup pricing, involves calculating total costs, then applying a markup percentage to those costs to reach an asking price. Retail brands aim for a 30-50% profit margin.
