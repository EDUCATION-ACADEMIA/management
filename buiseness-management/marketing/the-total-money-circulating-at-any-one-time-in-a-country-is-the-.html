The total money circulating at any one time in a country is the ____

(A) ** money supply

(B) monetary policy

(C) commerce

(D) Federal Reserve

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The money supply is the sum total of all of the currency and other liquid assets in a country’s economy on the date measured. The money supply includes all cash in circulation and all bank deposits that the account holder can easily convert to cash.

Concept note-2: -The money supply is the total amount of money-cash, coins, and balances in bank accounts-in circulation. The money supply is commonly defined to be a group of safe assets that households and businesses can use to make payments or to hold as short-term investments.

Concept note-3: -Currency in circulation is all of the money that has been issued by a country’s monetary authority, minus cash that has been removed from the system. Currency in circulation represents part of the overall money supply, with a portion of the overall supply being stored in checking and savings accounts.

Concept note-4: -Definition: The total stock of money circulating in an economy is the money supply. The circulating money involves the currency, printed notes, money in the deposit accounts and in the form of other liquid assets.
