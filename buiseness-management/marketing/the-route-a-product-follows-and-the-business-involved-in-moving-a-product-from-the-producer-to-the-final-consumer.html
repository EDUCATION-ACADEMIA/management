The route a product follows and the business involved in moving a product from the producer to the final consumer.

(A) distribution

(B) ** channel of distribution

(C) personal selling

(D) merchandising

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A distribution channel is a network of intermediaries that facilitates product delivery from the manufacturer to the end consumer and transfers payments from the buyer to the producer. In other words, it is the route through which a product travels from the production end to the point of consumption.

Concept note-2: -A distribution channel is the network of businesses or intermediaries through which a good or service passes until it reaches the end consumer. Distribution channels can contain many levels or intermediaries, such as wholesalers or retailers, as products move from manufacturer to consumer.

Concept note-3: -A distribution channel, in simple terms, is the flow that a good or service follows from production or manufacturing to the final consumer/buyer. The link between producers and the end consumer is normally intermediaries, such as wholesalers, retailers, or brokers.

Concept note-4: -Stanton: – “A channel of distribution (sometimes also called trade channel) for a product is the route taken by the title to the goods as they move from the producer to the ultimate customer or industrial users.”

Concept note-5: -A direct distribution channel is organized and managed by a company that sells directly to consumers. In such a case, the company keeps all aspects of delivery in-house (instead of using vendors) and is solely responsible for ensuring that customers receive their purchases successfully.
