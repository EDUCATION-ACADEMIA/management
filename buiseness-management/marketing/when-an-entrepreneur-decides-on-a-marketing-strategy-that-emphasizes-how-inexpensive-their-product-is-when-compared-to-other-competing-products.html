When an entrepreneur decides on a marketing strategy that emphasizes how inexpensive their product is when compared to other competing products:

(A) non-sales competition

(B) sales competition

(C) ** price competition

(D) non-price competition

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What are the 4Ps of marketing? (Marketing mix explained) The four Ps are product, price, place, and promotion. They are an example of a “marketing mix, ” or the combined tools and methodologies used by marketers to achieve their marketing objectives.

Concept note-2: -A marketing strategy refers to a business’s overall game plan for reaching prospective consumers and turning them into customers of their products or services. A marketing strategy contains the company’s value proposition, key brand messaging, data on target customer demographics, and other high-level elements.

Concept note-3: -A pull marketing strategy, also called a pull promotional strategy, refers to a strategy in which a firm aims to increase the demand for its products and draw (“pull”) consumers to the product. Pull marketing strategies revolve around getting consumers to want a particular product.

Concept note-4: -A brand leveraging strategy uses the power of an existing brand name to support a company’s entry into a new, but related, product category.
