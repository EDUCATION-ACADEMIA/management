What does the overall marketing mix of a firm determine?

(A) ** Marketing strategy

(B) Marketing objective

(C) Profit from marketing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The marketing mix in marketing strategy: Product, price, place and promotion. The marketing mix is the set of controllable, tactical marketing tools that a company uses to produce a desired response from its target market. It consists of everything that a company can do to influence demand for its product.

Concept note-2: -Importance of Marketing Mix Helps understand what your product or service can offer to your customers. Helps plan a successful product offering. Helps with planning, developing and executing effective marketing strategies. Helps businesses make use of their strengths and avoid unnecessary costs.

Concept note-3: -A marketing mix can consist of any combination of factors, but most commonly refers to what is known as the 4 Ps of marketing: product, price, promotion and place. Each of these four Ps can influence a consumer’s decision-making.

Concept note-4: -The four Ps are a “marketing mix” comprised of four key elements-product, price, place, and promotion-used when marketing a product or service. Typically, businesses consider the four Ps when creating marketing plans and strategies to effectively market to their target audience.

Concept note-5: -A company’s marketing mix is the combination of products, pricing, places and promotions it uses to differentiate itself from the competition. These four elements are commonly referred to as the “four Ps.” There is strong dependency between each of the Ps.
