Tangible goods, such as a baseball, or a cellphone.

(A) services

(B) wants

(C) ** product

(D) needs

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Tangible assets are physical things. Examples include land, buildings, vehicles, furniture, and equipment.

Concept note-2: –A tangible feature is something that can satisfy a prospect’s identified need. Tangible features are the facts that your prospects logically use to justify their emotional decisions. They include the descriptive characteristics of your product or service and the elements of your offer, including incentives.

Concept note-3: -In marketing, the word “tangible” refers to things that are physical, that is, items that can be touched, seen, heard or smelled. Tangible marketing is the use of promotional items to contribute to brand recognition and customer loyalty.

Concept note-4: -The phone won’t operate without an operating system, though – hence the name – and both iOS and Android are intangible goods.
