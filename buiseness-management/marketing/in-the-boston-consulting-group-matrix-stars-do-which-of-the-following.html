In the Boston Consulting Group matrix, stars do which of the following?

(A) Achieve high market growth in the future but currently, have low market share

(B) ** Hold a sizeable portion of a small, but growing market

(C) Hold high market share in a mature market

(D) Hold a low market share in a mature market

(E) Hold low market share in a declining market

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Boston Consulting group’s product portfolio matrix (BCG matrix) is designed to help with long-term strategic planning, to help a business consider growth opportunities by reviewing its portfolio of products to decide where to invest, to discontinue, or develop products. It’s also known as the Growth/Share Matrix.

Concept note-2: -The BCG growth-share matrix contains four distinct categories: “dogs, ” “cash cows, ” “stars, ” and “question marks.”

Concept note-3: -The BCG Matrix: Stars Products in the stars quadrant are market-leading products and require significant investment to retain their market position, boost growth, and maintain a competitive advantage. Stars consume a significant amount of cash but also generate large cash flows.
