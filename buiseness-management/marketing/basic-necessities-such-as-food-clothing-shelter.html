Basic necessities such as food, clothing, shelter.

(A) wants

(B) ** needs

(C) tangible

(D) intangible

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Many modern lists emphasize the minimum level of consumption of “basic needs” of not just food, water, clothing and shelter, but also transportation (as proposed in the Third talk of Livelihood section of Three Principles of the People) sanitation, education, and healthcare.

Concept note-2: -Maslow used the terms “physiological", “safety", “belonging and love", “social needs” or “esteem", “self-actualization” and “transcendence” to describe the pattern through which human needs and motivations generally move.

Concept note-3: -We must have food, water, air, and shelter to survive. If any one of these basic needs is not met, then humans cannot survive.

Concept note-4: -Food to eat, clothing to wear, and shelter from the elements. Aside from clothing, food and shelter are the same basic needs that every non-swimming living thing on this planet requires. Without sustenance and a place to sleep and protect oneself from environmental conditions, it is almost impossible to live.
