What is reliability?

(A) A set of questions fully answering what the business wants to know about a target market

(B) When a research technique produces results that are not correlated

(C) When you can count on a company to perform well in the upcoming year

(D) ** When a research technique produces nearly identical results in repeated trials

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Reliability * exists when a research technique produces nearly identical results in repeated trials. It requires that the questions ask for the same type of information from all respondents. When questions asked on a questionnaire measure what was intended to be asked.

Concept note-2: -In probability sampling, the units are selected randomly while in quota sampling a non-random method is used-it is usually left up to the interviewer to decide who is sampled.

Concept note-3: -Qualitative research uses several techniques including interviews, focus groups, and observation. [1][2][3] Interviews may be unstructured, with open-ended questions on a topic and the interviewer adapts to the responses.

Concept note-4: -In other words, secondary data refers to data that have already been collected for some other purpose.

Concept note-5: -A primary data source is an original data source, that is, one in which the data are collected firsthand by the researcher for a specific research purpose or project.
