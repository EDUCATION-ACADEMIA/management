What is the purpose of performing a SWOT Analysis?

(A) ** Identifies internal and external factors that may affect the business future performance.

(B) Evaluates whether the business venture is a good idea.

(C) Access a organization’s performance

(D) Gives the management team a broader view on the potential opportunity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A SWOT analysis helps you assess internal factors that might affect your business (strengths and weaknesses) and external factors (opportunities and threats). You will need to review and act on the results from the SWOT analysis.

Concept note-2: -A SWOT analysis is a strategic planning tool used to assess the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats of your business. Developing a SWOT analysis can help you look at your business in a new way and from different directions. It can also help you to: create or fine tune your business strategy.

Concept note-3: -The internal factors are strengths and weaknesses; the external factors are opportunities and threats. A SWOT analysis gives an organization a clear picture of the “situation” in which it operates and helps it identify which strategies to pursue.

Concept note-4: -A SWOT analysis is designed to facilitate a realistic, fact-based, data-driven look at the strengths and weaknesses of an organization, initiatives, or within its industry. The organization needs to keep the analysis accurate by avoiding pre-conceived beliefs or gray areas and instead focusing on real-life contexts.

Concept note-5: -SWOT analysis is one very effective tool for the analysis of environmental data and information – for both, internal (strengths, weakness) and external (opportunities, threats) factors. It helps to minimize the effect of weaknesses in your business, while maximizing your strengths.
