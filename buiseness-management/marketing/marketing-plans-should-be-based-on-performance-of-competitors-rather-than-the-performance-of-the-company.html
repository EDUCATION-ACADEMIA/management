MARKETING

[SOURCES]
Marketing plans should be based on performance of competitors rather than the performance of the company.

(A) true

(B) ** false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Most Important Piece of a Marketing Plan Is Your Targeted Customer. Knowing and anticipating the wants and needs of your customers shapes all the other elements of your plan.

Concept note-2: -It includes SMART marketing goals, deadlines, action steps, long-term objectives, target audiences, core marketing messages, and metrics. The plan is detailed, yet scannable. By the end of it, one can walk away with a strong understanding of the organization’s strategic direction for their upcoming marketing efforts.

Concept note-3: -The purpose of a marketing plan is to define strategies to engage audiences in order to achieve business objectives. The goal of a marketing plan is to ensure marketing activities are structured, relevant, and timely to achieve an organization’s objectives.

Concept note-4: -A well-defined marketing strategy leads to an increase in the business sales and marketing. This directly impacts the revenue generated as the more you reach out to people, the more they reach out to your business or brand. Of course, the marketing of a business is directly related to the sales it generates.
