Market share can be described as

(A) The number of suppliers in a particular market

(B) ** The percentage of total sales in a market that can be attributed to a firm

(C) The relative size of a particular market

(D) The total sales, as measured by value or volume, in a market

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What Is Market Share? Market share is the percent of total sales in an industry generated by a particular company. Market share is calculated by taking the company’s sales over the period and dividing it by the total sales of the industry over the same period.

Concept note-2: -Market share is the percentage of an industry’s sales that a particular company owns. Essentially, it is the share of your business’s total industry revenue from selling your products and services. Businesses with larger market shares are industry leaders and competition for smaller companies.

Concept note-3: -A company’s market share is its sales measured as a percentage of an industry’s total revenues. You can determine a company’s market share by dividing its total sales or revenues by the industry’s total sales over a fiscal period. Use this measure to get a general idea of the size of a company relative to the industry.

Concept note-4: -Market share is the percentage of the total revenue or sales in a market that a company’s business makes up. For example, if there are 50, 000 units sold per year in a given industry, a company whose sales were 5, 000 of those units would have a 10 percent share in that market.

Concept note-5: -Market share refers to the portion or percentage of a market earned by a company or an organization. In other words, a company’s market share is its total sales in relation to the overall industry sales of the industry in which it operates.
